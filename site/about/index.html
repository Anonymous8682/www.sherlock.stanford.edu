About

Need access to compute resources to support your sponsored or departmental research? Your laptop overheats? Your lab's workstations don't fit the bill anymore? You may want to try out the Stanford Sherlock cluster.

Purchased and supported with seed funding from Stanford's Provost, Sherlock comprises 127 compute servers and associated storage. Those 127 servers are available for use by all Stanford faculty and their research teams, to run their computational codes and programs, with resources managed through a job scheduler using a fair-share algorithm.

The Stanford Research Computing Center also offers Faculty the possibility to purchase additional dedicated resources to augment Sherlock, by becoming Sherlock "owners". Choosing from a standard set of server configurations supported by the SRCC staff, owners' servers are "joined" to the base Sherlock cluster. "Owners" have access to the base cluster as before, through fair-share. But they also have priority access to the resources they purchased, whenever they want. When an owner's servers aren't in use, other owners can use them ... but non-owners cannot. The base Sherlock configuration is 127 servers; since June 2014, an additional 600 servers have been added by owners.

You can learn more about Sherlock by visiting http://sherlock.stanford.edu .

Information on Sherlock's GPU servers can be found here.

Sherlock is a shared compute cluster available for use by all Stanford faculty and their research teams. Using the SLURM resources manager, all research teams have access to a base set of about 120 general compute nodes, 5 GPU-based servers, 2 "large-memory" servers with 1.5TB, and a 2.5 PB Lustre parallel file system for temporary files.

Faculty can supplement the base nodes by purchasing additional servers, thus becoming Sherlock owners. Thus far, owners have added more than 600 new servers to Sherlock, servers available to their teams and other owners.
